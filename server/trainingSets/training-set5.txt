Christian Miler and Kem Armstrong’s story, called “<a href="https://www.themarshallproject.org/2015/12/16/an-unbelievable-story-of-rape?ref=hp-2-112#.nXDOj42tQ">An Unbelievable Story of Rape</a>,” went on to win the Pulitzer Prize.</div></div></div><div class="after-section-content"></div><div class="section-outer-container"><div class="section-container section-text-container"><div class="section-text">Ironically, <em>Unbelievable </em>is a very accurate depiction of a story that nobody believed at the time. In 2008, an 18-year-old from Lynnwood, Washington claimed that she was raped by an intruder in the middle of the night. In conversations with trusted adults and an ex-boyfriend, Marie (Kaitlyn Dever in <em>Unbelievable</em>) explained that a stranger had snuck into her bedroom, blindfolded her, raped at knifepoint for hours, then left.&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="after-section-content"><div class="section-ad"><span>Advertisement</span><div class="ad" data-ad-size="1280x90,728x90,300x250,10x10" data-ad-position="A" id="ad-section-ad-A"></div></div></div><div class="section-outer-container"><div class="section-container section-text-container"><div class="section-text">But when police detectives, two sets of foster parents, and her ex-boyfriend were all vocally suspicious of her story, Marie began to doubt herself. Did she imagine the encounter, as they suggested? Was the traumatic event just something she dreamed up for attention?&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="after-section-content"><div class="ad native-ad" data-ad-size="fluid" data-ad-position="native" id="ad-native-add"></div></div><div class="section-outer-container"><div class="section-container section-text-container"><div class="section-text">Marie ran into a problem unique to rape cases: The credibility of the victim tends to be on trial, too. Detectives Jeffrey Mason and his partner, Jerry Rittgarn, were focused more on Marie than on the felony crime. A later report filed by a sex crime specialist <a href="https://www.thisamericanlife.org/581/transcript">Sergeant Greg Rinta concluded</a>, "The manner in which she was treated by Sergeant Mason and Detective Rittgarn can only be labeled as bullying and coercive," according to reporting done by<a href="https://www.thisamericanlife.org/581/transcript"> </a><em><a href="https://www.thisamericanlife.org/581/transcript">This American Life </a></em><a href="https://www.thisamericanlife.org/581/transcript">and </a><em><a href="https://www.thisamericanlife.org/581/transcript">The Marshall Project</a></em>.</div></div></div><div class="after-section-content"></div><div class="section-outer-container"><div class="section-container section-text-container"><div class="section-text">After meeting with the police, Marie’s faith in herself was so shaken that she recanted her story.
