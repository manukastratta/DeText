<p><b>Wood</b> is a porous and fibrous structural tissue found in the <a href="/wiki/Plant_stem" title="Plant stem">stems</a> and roots of <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">trees</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Woody_plant" title="Woody plant">woody plants</a>. It is an <a href="/wiki/Organic_material" class="mw-redirect" title="Organic material">organic material</a> - a natural <a href="/wiki/Composite_material" title="Composite material">composite</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cellulose" title="Cellulose">cellulose</a> fibers that are strong in tension and embedded in a <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/matrix" class="extiw" title="wikt:matrix">matrix</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lignin" title="Lignin">lignin</a> that resists compression. Wood is sometimes defined as only the secondary <a href="/wiki/Xylem" title="Xylem">xylem</a> in the stems of trees,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> or it is defined more broadly to include the same type of tissue elsewhere such as in the roots of trees or shrubs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In a living tree it performs a support function, enabling woody plants to grow large or to stand up by themselves. It also conveys water and <a href="/wiki/Nutrient" title="Nutrient">nutrients</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a>, other growing tissues, and the roots. Wood may also refer to other plant materials with comparable properties, and to material engineered from wood, or wood chips or fiber.
</p><p>Wood has been used for thousands of years for <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuel</a>, as a <a href="/wiki/Construction_material" class="mw-redirect" title="Construction material">construction material</a>, for making <a href="/wiki/Tool" title="Tool">tools</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weapon" title="Weapon">weapons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Furniture" title="Furniture">furniture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a>. More recently it emerged as a feedstock for the production of purified cellulose and its derivatives, such as <a href="/wiki/Cellophane" title="Cellophane">cellophane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cellulose_acetate#production" title="Cellulose acetate">cellulose acetate</a>.
</p><p>As of 2005, the growing stock of <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a> worldwide was about 434 billion cubic meters, 47% of which was commercial.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As an abundant, <a href="/wiki/Carbon-neutral" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon-neutral">carbon-neutral</a> renewable resource, woody materials have been of intense interest as a source of renewable energy. In 1991 approximately 3.5 billion cubic meters of wood were harvested. Dominant uses were for furniture and building construction.<sup id="cite_ref-Ullmann_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ullmann-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
